No Oil Air Fried Turkey

Ingredients

  • Oil Free Electric Roaster
  • Apple wood chips soaked in water
  • 16 to 20 pound turkey cleaned dried with giblets and neck removed, thawed.
  • quarters of butter at room temperature
  • turkey seasoning
  • kosher salt and pepper
  • 3 celery stalks
  • 1 onion with skin
  • giblets and turkey neck
  • 1/4 cup all purpose flour
  1. Combine a quarter of room temperature butter with kosher salt, pepper and turkey seasoning to make a paste to cover the turkey and under the skin.
  2. Tuck the wings under itself so they do not burn.
  3. The size of the turkey will determine the cooking time. Place a meat thermometer into the thickest part of the bird.
  4. The calculations for cooking time is as follows: 10 minutes for every weight listed on the tag.
  5. 16 lb x 10 = 160 minutes or 2 hours and 40 minutes.
  6. The meat thermometer must be oven proof and will remain in the bird while cooking.
  7. The turkey must be 165 degrees F internal temperature to ensure that it is safe for consumption
  8. Place wood chips in the bottom of the machine. Keep an eye on the wood chips and replace when necessary.
  9. Place the turkey in the machine and set control to high. Put the timer on.
  10. This is an outdoor machine. Do not cook in the home or garage.
  11. Place the pan under the machine for the pan drippings. Do not throw this away. You will make a gravy from the pan drippings.
  12. Start the turkey stock. Place the giblets, neck, onion with skin, celery, turkey seasoning, and pepper into a saucepan with water on medium heat.
  13. Let the stock cook for 1-2 hours and lower the heat to simmer.
  14. Strain the stock for the pan drippings.
  15. Check the turkey, pan drippings, wood chips, and internal temperature.
  16. Retrieve the turkey and pan drippings when the timer goes off and or the internal temperature reaches 165 degrees F.
  17. Attempt to remove the fat from the pan drippings.
  18. Make a roux from the flour and 1/4 cup of reduced stock in a saucepan. Keep stirring so there are no lumps.
  19. Pour the pan drippings into the roux and reduced stock mixture. If more stock is necessary, add more stock. Season to taste.
  20. Allow the turkey to rest at least 20 minutes before carving.
  21. Serve with homemade gravy and Potatoes Anne.
